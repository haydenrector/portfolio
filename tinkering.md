For years I've been exploring engineering and science with classes and home projects. These are some of my favorites:

## Digital Logic Projects
### ClockClock
This project is my favorite out of everything I have made. Through this one project alone, I learned how to 3D model/print small gears and mechanisms, laser cut, and use Verilog to operate stepper motors.

I used Verilog and an FPGA to design the circuit for the clock. My Verilog code for this project is in my Digital-Logic repo.

<img src="https://i.imgur.com/Sae5slb.jpeg" width="300">

Had to get crafty as I worked with what I had (hot glue and duck tape), but the final product turned out looking like a solid prototype. 

<img src="https://i.imgur.com/vVdmk1E.jpeg" width="300">

<img src="https://i.imgur.com/DRN3p9D.jpeg" width="300">

Some errors and issues I ran into with this project was getting each stepper motor to simultaneously move. In the end, I only had one small analog stepper motor moving at the correct speed. However, I am excited to continue this project in the future to achieve a fully functional ClockClock.
